Bornali Phukan

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the Beckman Institute, University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, working in the Statistical Speech Technology group under Mark Hasegawa-Johnson. My research is focused on advancing speech recognition technology, with an emphasis on improving accessibility for individuals with diverse speech patterns and disabilities. I have contributed to projects that fine-tune ASR models, improving their effectiveness for people with dysarthria and other speech disorders.

Passionate about combining AI, Natural Language Processing (NLP), and Machine Learning, I aim to enhance user experiences and promote inclusivity. My recent work integrates Large Language Models (LLMs) with ASR systems to correct transcription errors in challenging contexts like dysarthric speech. I have co-authored several papers, including one that will be presented at INTERSPEECH 2024, addressing innovative strategies for speech technology accessibility.
